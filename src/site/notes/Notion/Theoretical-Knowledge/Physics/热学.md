# Avogadro’s Number

## The Distribution of Molecular Speed

The Maxwell speed distribution P(v) is a function such that P(v)dv gives the  
fraction of molecules with speeds in the interval dv at speed v :  

$$P(v) = 4\pi v^2 \left(\frac{M}{2\pi RT}\right)^{3/2} e^{\frac{-Mv^2}{2RT}}$$

Due to Bolzmann distribution :

$$f(\vec{v})dv_xdv_ydv_z∝ e^{\frac{-Mv^2}{2RT}}dv_xdv_ydv_z$$

$$\dfrac{m}{k}=\dfrac MR$$

$$1=\int^\infty_0P(v)dv=4\pi A\int^\infty_0e^{\frac{-mv^2}{2kT}}v^2dv$$

As we know :

$$\int^\infty_0x^{2n}e^{-ax^2}dx=\dfrac{(2n-1)!!}{2^{n+1}a^n}\sqrt\dfrac\pi a$$

So due to n = 1 :

$$A=\left(\dfrac{M}{2\pi RT}\right)^\frac32$$

  

## The Distribution of Molecular Speed

$$v_{\text{avg}}=\int^\infty_0vP(v)dv=\sqrt\dfrac{8RT}{\pi M}\ (\text{average speed})$$

$$v_{\text{rms}}=\sqrt{\int^\infty_0v^2P(v)dv}=\sqrt\dfrac{3RT}{M}\ (\text{rms speed})$$

The most probable speed (vp) happens when dP/dv=0, so :

$$2ve^{\frac{-mv^2}{2RT}}+v^2e^{\frac{-mv^2}{2RT}}\left(-\dfrac{mv}{RT}\right)=0$$

$$v_p=\sqrt\dfrac{2RT}{M}$$
